§ 08 — Principals
Leadership

Senior principals commit directly to every transaction and partnership relationship.

Aurora Resources Zambia · Reg.

Philip John Routledge

Director · Principal

Experienced mining entrepreneur and trading executive with over 30 years across Africa and beyond. Born in Newcastle, UK, raised in Zambia, his career spans logistics, explosives manufacturing, mining innovation, and international mineral trading.

After early work in South Africa's logistics sector, Philip founded Rocktech Group in 1997, where he developed and patented a low-energy explosive system for production mining. His career has taken him across Africa, South America, Asia, and Europe — managing projects in Kenya, Rwanda, Ghana, Sierra Leone, Guinea, Burundi, the DRC, Colombia, and Brazil.

In 2017 he established Aurora Resources DMCC in Dubai, expanding into mineral trading with a focus on copper, cobalt, rare earths, cassiterite, and PGMs. In 2021 he relocated the business to Zambia, strengthening his presence in the Copperbelt region and the DRC.

Today Philip is based in Ndola, Zambia, near the Congo border, leveraging his network and local knowledge to drive growth in mineral trading.

Chekina SARL DRC · Reg.

Jean-Claude Kunda Bwanga

Managing Director

Seasoned mining professional and business leader based in the DRC. Extensive expertise in the mining sector, with a pivotal role in promoting and developing artisanal and small-scale mining activities in the Katanga region.

As Managing Director of Chekina SARL, Mr. Kunda Bwanga has advanced the company's operations and partnerships, contributing to the sustainability of the mining industry. His work emphasises organisation of mining cooperatives and responsible mining practices.

As Vice President of the Congo–Poland Chamber of Commerce, he fosters economic cooperation between the DRC and international partners, strengthening cross-border institutional collaboration.

Recognised for his entrepreneurial vision and commitment to sustainable development, Mr. Kunda Bwanga continues to champion initiatives that enhance the mining sector's contribution to economic growth.

§ 09 — FAQ
Buyer & investor questions

The questions serious counterparties ask first.

Every lot is tracked from source through refining, transport, and loadout — with assay documentation, weight certificates, transport manifests, and export clearance forming a continuous documentation chain. Independent inspection is appointed per shipment where contract requires.
Minimum volumes are negotiated per contract and depend on the delivery structure (FOB / CIF) and production schedule. Trial shipments can be structured for first-time counterparties.
Lead times span production allocation, inspection, inland transit through the Ndola corridor to Dar es Salaam, and sea voyage to the destination port. Exact timings are confirmed per contract based on production slot and vessel schedule.
SBLC and TT structures via top-tier banks. Instruments are authenticated through Aurora Resources' counterparty banking arrangements before production allocation and loading.
Risk allocation follows Incoterms 2020: under FOB, title and risk pass at the port of loading; under CIF, the seller arranges carriage and insurance to the destination port, with risk passing at the loading port. The full Incoterm definition and any contract-specific variations are set out in the sale contract.
Counterparties are screened against OFAC, EU, UK, and UN sanctions lists. KYC and AML procedures follow FATF guidance; source-of-funds and end-use declarations are collected where required. No dealings with sanctioned entities.
Yes — qualified counterparties are welcome to visit operations after KYC completion and subject to safety and access protocols. Visits are coordinated through Aurora Resources' Ndola office.
